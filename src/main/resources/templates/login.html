The Importance of Art

Art is an expression of our inner thoughts, feelings, and experiences. It's also an expression of creativity that can be used for self-reflection or social influence. Art movements have often played important roles in social changes, and should be fully integrated into our lives, our community and education in general. Painting, architecture, art sculpture, music, literature, and many other fine arts can be considered a repository of a given society's collective memory.

As the The Metropolitan Museum of Art puts it: “Looking at art from the past contributes to who we are as people. By looking at what has been done before, we gather knowledge and inspiration that contribute to how we speak, feel, and view the world around us.” In addition to providing commentary about the larger culture, art makes life more manageable, tolerable and enjoyable.

The experience of art is inseparable from travel.

In the words of Leo Tolstoy: The activity of art is based on the capacity of people to infect others with their own emotions and to be infected by the emotions of others. Strong emotions, weak emotions, important emotions or irrelevant emotions, good emotions or bad emotions – if they contaminate the reader, the spectator, or the listener – it attains the function of art. In sum, art can be considered important because It accesses higher orders of thinking. Art doesn’t just make you absorb information. Rather, it makes you think about current ideas and inspire you to make your own. This is why creativity is a form of intelligence – it is a special ability that unlocks the potentials of the human mind. In fact, studies have shown that exposure to art can make you better in other fields of knowledge.
